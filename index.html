💸 Finance

Why Your Savings Account Is Actually a Joke

Your bank gives you 3% interest. Inflation runs at 6%. Congratulations — you are officially getting poorer while doing nothing. That's not saving, that's a slow financial funeral with a savings passbook as the eulogy. The real game? Assets. Index funds. Gold. Real estate. Anything that outpaces inflation. Yet here we are, letting banks hold our money hostage for pocket change. Time to wake up, desi investor.

📊 Finance

Crypto: Genius Investment or Expensive Gambling?

Bitcoin made millionaires. Bitcoin also made a lot of people cry into their keyboards at 2am. The truth is somewhere in between — it's a volatile, speculative, occasionally revolutionary asset class that is 100% not for the faint-hearted. My hot take? Allocate only what you can afford to watch go to zero, laugh about it, and still afford chai. Because crypto doesn't care about your feelings.

🏦 Finance

SIP or Skip? The Truth About Mutual Funds in India

Everyone's uncle who works in finance will tell you to "start a SIP." And honestly? They're not wrong — they're just incomplete. SIPs are great for long-term wealth, but mutual funds aren't magic. Expense ratios eat returns. Fund managers are human. Markets go south. Still, for the average person with a job, a dream, and zero time to watch the Nifty 50, a SIP in an index fund might just be the smartest boring decision you ever make.

🗳️ Politics

Election Season: Where Promises Go to Die

Every 5 years, politicians rediscover the poor. Suddenly there are roads to be built, gas cylinders to be given, and photographs to be taken next to farmers. Then the election ends and — poof — they vanish like they were never there. It's the greatest magic trick in democracy: making voters' problems disappear while making politicians' bank accounts appear. We deserve better. Or at least more creative lies.

News Anchors vs Journalists: What's the Difference?

Once upon a time, journalism meant holding power accountable. Today? It means shouting louder than the person opposite you on a primetime debate. The news anchor has evolved from reporter to performer, complete with dramatic music, red banners, and outrage calibrated to exact TRP frequencies. Meanwhile, actual investigative journalists file RTIs from laptops and get about 1,200 views. The world is fine.

🧑‍💼 Politics

The Art of the Political U-Turn

Yesterday they were against it. Today they're for it. Tomorrow depends on opinion polls. The political U-turn is India's most practised sport — no training required, no shame needed. Coalition demands, constituency pressure, or just vibes can change a politician's position faster than a F1 car at Monza. Principles are optional. Adaptability is mandatory. And voters? Mostly forgiving. Which says more about us than them.

⚔️ War

Why Do Wars Start? (Hint: It's Never Just One Reason)

History teachers will give you one clean answer. Reality gives you seventeen messy ones. Wars start because of oil, egos, borders, religion, propaganda, fear, greed, and the occasional assassination of an archduke. The frightening part is how normal it all sounds once you peel back the nationalism. Ordinary people, convinced that dying for a politician's decision is glory. The 21st century hasn't fixed this. It just upgraded the weapons.

🕊️ War

Arms Dealers: The World's Most Politely Ignored Industry

The global arms trade is worth over $600 billion a year. Countries that preach peace sell weapons to countries at war. Nobody blinks. Defence stocks go up when conflicts start. Nobody blinks again. We have conferences on climate change, summits on poverty, conventions on human rights — and absolutely nobody wants to seriously talk about who's supplying the weapons making all of that worse. Follow the money. Always follow the money.

📉 Economics

Inflation Explained: Why Your ₹100 Buys Less Every Year

In 2000, ₹100 could feed a small village. Today it barely covers a café sandwich. Welcome to inflation — the invisible tax that nobody voted for but everyone pays. Central banks print money to stimulate growth, costs rise, salaries lag, and somehow the answer is always "cut your avocado toast." The real issue is structural: wages haven't kept up with asset prices in decades. That's not personal failure. That's policy.

🌍 Economics

Is India Actually a $5 Trillion Economy? Let's Be Real.

The headlines say India is booming. The GDP charts look great. But ask a first-generation graduate struggling to find a job in their field, or a farmer who's seen input costs triple — and "booming" starts to sound hollow. GDP is a measure of total output, not a measure of how well the average person is doing. India's growth story is real, but so is its inequality story. Both can be true. In fact, they must both be told.

💊 Health

Sleep Is the Original Superfood (And You're Starving)

You will spend ₹3,000 on a protein powder that tastes like chalk but won't go to bed before 1am. That's the modern health paradox. Science is pretty clear: 7-9 hours of quality sleep improves memory, metabolism, immunity, mood, and probably your personality. And it's free. But we've created a culture where exhaustion is a badge of honour and rest is laziness. Wear your sleep like a trophy. Your brain is begging you.

🧠 Health

Mental Health in India: Finally Talking, Slowly Healing

For decades, mental health was the family secret — the thing whispered about, never treated. "Just pray more." "Stress toh hoga." "Be strong." The result? A silent epidemic hidden behind high-functioning facades. The good news is that the conversation is finally beginning — driven by young people who refuse to suffer quietly. The bad news is that therapists are still unaffordable for most. Awareness without access is just inspiration porn. We need both.

🍛 Food

The Biryani Wars: Hyderabadi vs Lucknowi (I Chose Violence)

This is not a debate. This is a matter of identity, heritage, and deeply personal rice-related trauma. Hyderabadi biryani fans will tell you the dum process is sacred. Lucknowi fans will say pakki dum is the only civilised way to eat rice. Both are correct. Both are wrong. The real answer is: whichever biryani is in front of you right now is the best biryani. Except the overpriced hotel biryani. That one deserves criticism.

🥘 Food

Street Food vs Restaurant: Where Does India Really Eat?

The ₹30 pav bhaji at the corner stall hits differently than the ₹450 "deconstructed pav bhaji" at a hipster café. India eats on the streets — always has. The roadside thela is a cultural institution, a sociology paper, and a culinary school rolled into one. And yet we dismiss it as "unhygienic" while paying triple for air-conditioned mediocrity. The most authentic food in India has never needed a Zomato rating. It has a queue at 8pm. That's enough.

🥦 Veg

Going Veg in India: Easy Mode or Impossible Quest?

India is simultaneously the best and most confusing country to be vegetarian in. On one hand: paneer in every form imaginable, dal makhani that exists on a spiritual plane, samosas at every corner. On the other hand: "it's just chicken stock, not non-veg" at every restaurant, and the constant social interrogation of your dietary choices by people who are eating jalebi for breakfast. Being veg in India is an identity, not just a meal plan.

🌱 Veg

Plant-Based vs Traditional Veg: The New Debate Nobody Asked For

Now comes the plant-based meat — the burger that bleeds beet juice, the sausage made of soya, the "chicken" that has never met a chicken. Western startups are spending billions to make vegetables pretend to be meat, while India has centuries of vegetarian cuisine that needs no pretending. Dal doesn't need to bleed. Chana masala doesn't need a rebrand. We already have the world's most creative meatless food. We just forgot to charge $18 for it.

🕌 Religion

Faith, Festivals & the Business of Belief in India

India doesn't just have religion — it has a religion economy. Temples with more gold than small nations. Festival seasons that drive consumer spending. Astrology apps with millions of subscribers. We're a spiritual country that's also intensely capitalist about spirituality. And that's not necessarily wrong — it's just honest. The divine and the profitable have always walked hand in hand. At least in India, we're upfront about lighting a ₹500 diya for good returns.

🙏 Religion

Spiritual But Not Religious: Gen Z's New Default Setting

Millennials and Gen Z are increasingly ticking "spiritual but not religious" — lighting incense, reading Bhagavad Gita memes, attending sound baths, and also missing every Sunday prayer. They've kept the feeling, dropped the institution. Is that shallow? Or is it evolution? Religion was always personal before it became organised. Maybe we're returning to something older: the private, unmediated conversation with whatever is bigger than us. Or maybe it's just an aesthetic. Either way, the crystals are selling.
